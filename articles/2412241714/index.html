When life no longer feels like interacting with NPCs

2024/12/24

There are a few moments I’ve been reflecting on:

  1. Sitting on a bench at KFC, putting away my phone and waiting for my order, I looked at the electronic sign at the counter. Out of the corner of my eye, I saw the surroundings and suddenly felt like I was in a place far from home.
  2. Once, while waiting for the red light, I saw a garbage truck parked by the side of the road. Maybe it was the combination of the winter morning, the blue sky, and the sunlight, but it suddenly gave me the feeling of being in an unfamiliar city.

Describing this feeling is actually quite difficult. I’ve thought about it for a long time but haven’t found a fitting way to express it. Perhaps it’s the sense of novelty when you enter a new environment, but it’s not quite the same as freshness. Instead, it’s a feeling that blends familiarity and unfamiliarity.

The connection I can think of is from a trip I took to Tokyo earlier this year. I really liked the place, including its atmosphere. Maybe because I’m in an Asian country, even though I’m in a different nation with different cultures, people, and customs, I felt a sense of belonging. It’s as if we share a broader cultural background or similar living habits, so everything I saw felt both familiar and unfamiliar at the same time.

Honestly, I’m fascinated by this feeling. Gradually, I’ve come to define the meaning of travel in my own way.

You know when you find yourself in a new place, whether it’s a small village, a narrow road, or a shop you’ve never visited. Every corner you explore, every view you take in, could be both the first and the last time you see it. Behind this, things continue—like how the shopkeeper, after you leave, will finish their shift and live their own life. It feels both close and distant at the same time, creating a strange sensation, as if you’re looking at someone else’s world from a third-party perspective, with a sense of detachment, almost like an out-of-body experience.

Daily interactions, on the other hand, feel like chatting with NPCs due to their familiarity. After a few exchanges, it’s over, and there’s no thought about what comes next.

I think I should change this state of mind.
