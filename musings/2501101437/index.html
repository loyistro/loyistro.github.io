The rule of not following is the struggle for resources

Jan 11, 2025   ·   2 min read   ·   #Aha #Life

I am a “rule follower.” I enjoy the sense of order that rules bring because when things are done according to a specific order, the results feel predictable and under control, aligning with my expectations.

However, in the environment I live in, rule violations are still a frequent occurrence.

For instance, while waiting at a red light, motorbikes, bicycles, or pedestrians often cross before the light turns green. When the light changes, the lack of separation between pedestrians and vehicles leads to a chaotic and dangerous mingling of traffic.

Another example occurs near the school gates during drop-off times. Parents bringing their children to school—whether on foot, by motorbike, or by bicycle—crowd onto narrow sidewalks, creating situations just as unsafe as crossing when the light is green.

At first glance, it seems that the lack of proper pedestrian-vehicle separation in public infrastructure causes such chaos. However, I believe there is another underlying reason tied to this environment: the struggle for resources.

There are so many people living here, yet resources like space and other essentials are always in short supply. This scarcity triggers a primal survival instinct—one that persists even in modern society—and manifests in various aspects of daily life.

If I don’t run the red light, I might miss something important due to time constraints. If I don’t take the shortcut on the sidewalk, I might have to take a much longer route to reach my destination. There are always countless justifications for breaking the rules.

But it’s the rule-followers who bear the consequences. You might gain a time advantage to accomplish something, but I may face danger or lose the opportunity to cross during the light cycle. As illustrated by this example, this behavior is becoming increasingly common. Even visitors from outside the area quickly develop similar impressions.

I believe there’s still much for people here to learn.
